Nerual Proxies for the Imprisoned


This project connects EEG data from the Neurosky Mindwave headset to the Processing software, and finally to Rhinoceros 3D. This data flow enables the use of EEG activity to inform the design process based on attention acuity. This thesis examines the prison as a theater of neurological potentials for the ideas speculated above, through designed cognitive stimulation guided by our new found knowledge of the brain. Returning to the generative intent of prisons can be brought about by a new literature of practice between architects and neuroscientist to address the ambivalent condition of contemporary imprisonment.
